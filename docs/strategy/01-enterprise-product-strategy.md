# Enterprise Product Strategy

**The Platform (EKB) - Neutral**

## Executive Summary

The current documentation describes a powerful engineering platform for hybrid operations (physical + digital + financial + AI), but lacks a clear, market-facing product strategy.

This strategy defines what we sell, to whom, why they care, and how we position against alternatives.

Derived from Layers 1-3 and Volumes, but re-oriented to customer outcomes and market categories.

## Core Offering

The Platform is an **Event & Project Operations Platform** that helps organizations plan, coordinate, execute, monitor, and audit real-world operations involving events, venues, projects, teams, logistics, schedules, resources, assets, vendors, finance, payments, approvals, and documentation.

Supporting capabilities (financial systems, AI assistance, operational intelligence) exist to make coordination, execution, and auditability reliable.

## Strategic Pillars (Product View)

1. Easier coordination of events, projects, teams, and resources.
2. Reliable execution with strong visibility and accountability.
3. Built-in auditability for operations (including financial handling).
4. Operational intelligence and safe AI assistance.
5. Extensibility without lock-in.

Supporting technical implementation details live in the internal Layers.

## Product Vision (Market View)

To be the trusted platform that organizations use to run events and projects with less chaos and more reliable outcomes.

## Roadmap Alignment (High Level)

Focus remains on making event and project operations easier and more reliable. Technical foundation (Layers) is already strong; product work emphasizes templates, onboarding, integrations, and intelligence that directly serve operators.

## Success Metrics (Product)

- Time to first value for running an event or project.
- Reduction in coordination friction (measured via feedback or dropped tasks).
- Audit / compliance pass rate for operations.
- Adoption of coordination and visibility features.
- Safe, useful AI assistance (measured by acceptance + no bypasses of controls).

## Risks & Mitigations

- Risk: Over-emphasizing internal architecture in public messaging. Mitigation: Keep Layer details in internal docs; lead with operational benefits.
- Risk: Category confusion with generic tools. Mitigation: Clear "Event & Project Operations Platform" positioning + benchmarks.

This strategy drives updates to product-facing documentation. Internal engineering remains in Layers.
