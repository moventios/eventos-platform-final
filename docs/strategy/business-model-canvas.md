# Business Model Canvas — Event & Project Operations Platform

**Version:** 1.0

## Customer Segments

- Primary: B2B organizations managing physical spaces, events, venues, logistics with complex operations and financial requirements.
- Secondary: Implementation partners, integrators, and enterprise IT teams.
- Tertiary: Developers and AI tool builders building on the platform.

## Value Propositions

- Coordinated system for planning and running events and projects.
- Reliable execution with visibility and accountability.
- Built-in support for finance, approvals, documentation, and auditability.
- Operational intelligence and safe AI assistance.
- Extensible for teams and partners.

## Channels

- Direct sales for enterprise.
- GitHub + documentation for developer-led adoption.
- Implementation partners.
- Content marketing and thought leadership (AEO/GEO focus).

## Customer Relationships

- Self-serve for smaller tenants via docs and APIs.
- Dedicated support and implementation for enterprise.
- Community and partner ecosystem.

## Revenue Streams

- Subscription (per tenant or usage-based for operations volume).
- Premium support and implementation services.
- Partner revenue share for integrations.
- Future: White-label or embedded capabilities.

## Key Resources

- The EKB itself (Layers + Volumes).
- Engineering team aligned to SSOT.
- Strong focus on immutable systems and RLS.
- AI/RAG capabilities.

## Key Activities

- Maintaining and evolving the three Layers.
- Building reference implementations aligned to EPXA.
- Community and developer relations.
- Ensuring financial and operational invariants.

## Key Partnerships

- OSS projects (PostgreSQL, pgvector, Trigger.dev, etc.).
- Payment providers (Xendit, Stripe, Midtrans).
- Cloud providers (Supabase, Vercel, etc.).
- Implementation and consulting partners.

## Cost Structure

- Engineering and architecture (core).
- AI infrastructure and RAG.
- Documentation and community.
- Compliance and security (high due to financial integrity).
