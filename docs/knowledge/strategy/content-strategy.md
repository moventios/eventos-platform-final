## 4. Content Formats & Specifications

### Long-form Guide (Gated)

- **Length:** 3,000–8,000 words
- **Structure:** Executive summary + 5–8 sections + checklist + CTA
- **Design:** Branded PDF with cover, table of contents, callout boxes
- **Gate:** Email required; company name for enterprise-targeted guides

### Blog Article (SEO-optimized)

- **Length:** 1,500–3,000 words (pillar) or 800–1,500 words (spoke)
- **Structure:** TL;DR summary at top → H2 sections → conclusion → CTA
- **SEO:** Target 1 primary keyword, 3–5 secondary. Include FAQ section at bottom.
- **Images:** Minimum 2 (featured + 1 screenshot/diagram)

### How-To Article (Help Center)

- **Length:** 300–800 words
- **Structure:** Goal statement → prerequisites → numbered steps → expected result
- **Style:** Active voice. One action per step. Screenshots for every non-obvious step.
- **Schema:** `HowTo` markup

### Video Tutorial

- **Length:** 3–8 minutes
- **Format:** Screen recording with voiceover. No face cam required.
- **Structure:** What you'll learn (30s) → step-by-step (main) → result (30s) → next step CTA
- **Accessibility:** Captions required. Transcript available.

### Customer Story

- **Length:** 600–1,200 words
- **Structure:** Company intro → challenge → why us → solution → results (with metrics)
- **Approval:** Customer must approve before publish
- **Assets:** Logo, photo of contact (optional), 1–2 metrics as pull quotes

### Changelog

- **Length:** 100–500 words per entry
- **Format:** Date + version → What's new (features) → What's improved (fixes/UX) → What's fixed (bugs)
- **Tone:** Clear, specific. "Added ability to filter bookings by venue" not "improvements to filtering."

## 5. Content Quality Standards

### Before Publishing (Checklist)

- [ ] Uses canonical terminology from Layer-1 (AccessPass, Booking, Facility — not improvised synonyms)
- [ ] No forbidden words: robust, seamless, powerful, revolutionary, game-changing
- [ ] No architecture jargon in customer-facing copy (immutable ledger → "full audit trail")
- [ ] Specific claims are verifiable (metrics sourced, dates accurate)
- [ ] CTA present and relevant to the content
- [ ] SEO meta: title + description written
- [ ] Schema markup applied (blog, how-to, FAQ)
- [ ] Internal links: at least 2 to relevant pages
- [ ] Accessibility: alt text on all images, headings hierarchical (H1→H2→H3)

### Voice Check

Ask: "Does this sound like a senior ops professional, or like a marketing brochure?"

- ✅ "Your team spent 3 hours reconciling that budget. You shouldn't have to."
- ❌ "Our powerful, seamless solution drives operational excellence."

## 6. Original Research Program

### Annual: State of Event Operations Report

**Goal:** Establish us as the leading authority on how operations teams actually work  
**Method:** Annual survey of 300–500 operations managers, event directors, and finance leads  
**Publish:** Q1 each year  
**Promote:** Gated download, PR outreach, webinar based on findings, 5+ blog articles using data

**Sample survey questions:**

- "How many tools does your team use to manage a single event?"
- "How long does post-event financial reconciliation take?"
- "What is the most common cause of budget overruns in your events?"
- "What percentage of your event bookings involve a venue conflict?"
- "How long does your team spend on manual coordination tasks per week?"

**Expected output:**

- 10+ quotable statistics ("The average operations team uses 6+ tools to manage a single event")
- Benchmark data that positions our platform as the solution
- Content that LLMs will cite (original, structured, authoritative)
